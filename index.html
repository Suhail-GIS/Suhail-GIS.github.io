About Me

I am Mohd Suhail, a postgraduate in Geography and a BS Computer Science student at the University of the People. My academic foundation includes degrees in History, Political Science, and Education, with a deep-rooted passion for geospatial technologies, climate change, and sustainable development.

I specialize in GIS and Remote Sensing applications and work extensively with tools like QGIS, ArcGIS Pro, and Google Earth Engine. My interests lie in land use/land cover change, environmental monitoring, and spatial data analysis to support decision-making for urban planning, water resource management, and climate resilience.

I have actively contributed to climate awareness and rural engagement through the National Service Scheme (NSS). During my NSS tenure, I organized and participated in environmental campaigns, afforestation drives, and climate awareness speeches in nearby villages. I also led rallies and debates on climate change and its impact on rural communities, aligning my service work with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Through both research and fieldwork, I aim to contribute meaningfully to organizations like the United Nations, UNEP, ESRI or NASA that focus on global environmental and developmental challenges.
